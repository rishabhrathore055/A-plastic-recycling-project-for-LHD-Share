How can I reuse the things I already have? Recycling is very important, but even better is reusing. Recycling actually uses energy, transport time, and fuel to get the items to where they need to go. If you can reuse an item yourself, that's more energy, time, and fuel saved, as well as less emissions produced. Go and check out the ideas sections for ideas on how you can reuse the recyclable items at your home.

Q: What is “Single Stream” recycling?

Single stream recycling is a technical term that describes the comingling of all recyclable materials (plastics, cardboard, paper, etc.) instead of being separating each item by type.

Q: What happens after a truck picks up my recycling?

Collected recyclables are sorted by people and machinery and then baled to sell back to manufacturers. Your recyclables become new products. Even if your community uses the same trucks for all collection, rest assured MN has strict restrictions on landfilling materials intended to be recycled. Just like any other system, success depends on many small actions and those actions all add up! Please, recycle with care and help reduce waste.

Q: How Can I Start Recycling?

Join your city's curbside recycling program. Curbside recycling programs pickup recycling from your home once or twice a week. In most areas, you'll need a designated recycling bin to participate. Contact your city's public works department for more information. Find out what are some recycling centers near you

Q: Why is it important to only put items that can be recycled in the recycling bin?

Putting items in the recycling bin that can’t be recycled can contaminate the recycling stream. After these unrecyclable items arrive at recycling centers, they can cause costly damage to the equipment. Additionally, after arriving at recycling centers, they must be sorted out and then sent to landfills, which raises costs for the facility. That is why it is important to check with your local recycling provider to ensure that they will accept certain items before placing them into a bin. Some items may also be accepted at retail locations or other at local recycling centers.

Q: What things can I recycle?

There are several households objects you can recycle. (Most glass can be recycled, but it is usually sorted according to color. ... Paper and Cardboard. Paper is perhaps the broadest category of recyclable materials that can go into your recycling bin) Check out the Things you Can recycle page for more information.

Q: Why are some items that look recyclable not accepted at my recycling facility?

Your local recycling facility might not accept all recyclable items. This is especially true with plastics. While plastic bottles are the most commonly recycled plastic products, other plastics may or may not be accepted in your area, so first check what your local recycling provider accepts. It is important to understand that the existence of a plastic resin code on the product does not guarantee that the product is recyclable in your area. Additionally, glass may not be accepted in some areas, so please confirm with your local provider.

Q: Can I recycle materials with food residue or does the material have to be perfectly clean?

Mostly: Plastic, metal and glass materials must be empty and rinsed clean of food debris before being recycled. Paper materials must be empty, clean and dry before being recycled. Wet paper/food-soiled paper products may be compostable.
